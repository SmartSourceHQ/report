Section 1.0 – Introduction

1.0 Introduction & Basis of Valuation

Valuation Standards

This report is an AI-assisted investment analysis aligned to UK lender valuation principles. It is not a formal RICS Red Book valuation and has not been prepared or signed by a registered valuer. The analysis adopts the concept of Market Value as commonly understood: the estimated amount for which a property should exchange between a willing buyer and a willing seller in an arm's-length transaction, after proper marketing, wherein the parties had each acted knowledgeably, prudently and without compulsion. Users requiring a formal valuation for lending or regulatory purposes should commission a RICS-registered valuer.

Definitions

Stabilised Market Value

Value assuming the property is let at market rent on standard terms. This is a ceiling value — it does not account for current vacancy, lease-up costs, or required capital expenditure.

Forced Sale / Restricted Value

Value subject to reduced marketing periods (90–180 days), reflecting rapid disposal scenarios. Applied for lender stress-testing. Typically 15–25% below stabilised MV.

Estimated Market Rent (EMR)

The estimated rental value achievable on the open market assuming a new letting on standard institutional lease terms. This is a gross figure before deduction of void periods, letting fees, or tenant incentives.

Reinstatement Cost

Insurance valuation representing the cost to rebuild the structure new to modern standards. Not directly used for security valuation but required for insurance purposes.

Valuation Approach

The primary approach adopted is the Income Approach (Income Capitalization), reflecting the property's income-producing potential. This method values the property based on the net rental income capitalised at an appropriate yield (All-Risks Yield, ARY).

Formula: Market Value = Market Rent ÷ ARY

For this property, the secondary approach (comparison approach using sales and rental evidence) has been used to calibrate the income assumptions and validate the income method result.

Assumptions & Caveats

  • Good Title: Assumed title is good and unencumbered. No searches or legal review conducted.
  • Physical Condition: Valuation based on visual inspection only. No intrusive surveys conducted. Structural defects, latent defects, asbestos, or contamination may exist.
  • Planning & Statutory: Assumed all necessary consents in place and property complies with current regulations. No verification of listed status, planning history, or building regulations approval.
  • Environmental: No Phase 1 environmental assessment conducted. Flood and subsidence data sourced from publicly available indexes.
  • Tenancy: Rental assumptions based on market evidence only. No negotiations with potential tenants conducted. Void periods may vary.
  • Market Conditions: Valuation reflects April 2026 market conditions. Future market movements will affect value.
Important Disclaimer

This valuation is prepared solely for the use of the named client and is not intended for third parties. The property should not be relied upon for any purpose other than stated. All valuations are subject to the assumptions and caveats listed above. Professional advice (surveyor, solicitor, accountant, tax advisor) should be sought before making any investment decision.
